Using the Right Prompts with ChatGPT for Better Results

If you’re a small business owner exploring AI tools, ChatGPT can be a game-changer. This AI-powered chatbot can help with everything from writing marketing copy to answering customer questions. But to get the best results, you need to know how to ask the right questions—a skill called prompting.

In this post, we’ll cover how to craft effective prompts that help ChatGPT deliver clear, relevant, and actionable responses. No jargon, no fluff—just practical tips you can use today.

Why Your Prompt Matters

Think of ChatGPT as a helpful assistant who needs clear instructions to do their best work. If your prompt is vague or incomplete, you’ll get vague or incomplete answers.

Example:

  • Vague prompt: “Tell me about marketing.”
  • Better prompt: “Give me 3 low-cost marketing ideas for a local coffee shop targeting young professionals.”

The second prompt gives ChatGPT more context and specifics, resulting in a much more useful answer.

5 Tips for Writing Better Prompts

1. Be Specific and Clear

The more detail you provide, the better ChatGPT can tailor its response.

  • Instead of: “Help me write an email.”
  • Try: “Write a friendly follow-up email to a customer who hasn’t responded in 3 days, reminding them about their pending order.”

2. Set the Tone and Style

Tell ChatGPT how you want the message to sound.

  • Formal or casual?
  • Professional or playful?
  • Short and concise or detailed?

Example:
“Write a concise and professional LinkedIn post about our new product launch.”

3. Include Examples or Formats

If you want your output in a specific format, say so!

  • Bullet points
  • Numbered lists
  • A short paragraph
  • A table

Example:
“List 5 benefits of our service in bullet points, each no longer than 10 words.”

4. Ask for Step-by-Step Guidance

ChatGPT can break down processes into manageable steps.

Example:
“Explain step-by-step how to set up an Instagram ad campaign for my handmade jewelry business.”

5. Use Follow-Up Prompts

If the first answer isn’t perfect, refine your prompt and ask again.

  • “Can you make it more casual?”
  • “Add statistics or data to support the points.”
  • “Give me a shorter version for Twitter.”

Prompt Examples for Small Business Owners

Here are some ready-to-use prompts tailored for small businesses:

  • Marketing:
    “Suggest 5 creative social media post ideas for a boutique bakery targeting millennials.”

  • Customer Service:
    “Write a polite response to a customer complaint about a delayed shipment.”

  • Product Descriptions:
    “Create a captivating product description for a handmade soy candle, emphasizing eco-friendliness.”

  • Business Planning:
    “Outline a simple 3-month action plan to increase local foot traffic to my retail store.”

  • Financial Tips:
    “List 4 easy ways to reduce monthly expenses for a small online business.”

Bonus: Avoid These Common Prompt Mistakes

  • Too vague: “Tell me about business.”
  • Too broad: “Write a marketing plan.”
  • No context: “Create a flyer.”
  • Ignoring tone: “Write a customer email.” (without specifying tone)
  • Expecting too much at once: Asking for long essays or complicated analysis in one prompt.
